# Workspace Architecture

## Purpose

A Workspace is the primary operational environment where AlpenTind performs work.

A Workspace is **not** a page.

A Workspace is **not** a form.

A Workspace is a place where users understand a situation, perform work and make professional decisions.

Every Workspace shall support operational awareness before interaction.

Users should immediately understand:

- Where am I?
- What is happening?
- What needs my attention?
- What should I do next?

## Workspace Principles

### WP-001 — Situation before interaction

The Workspace shall first help the user understand the current situation. Only then present actions.

### WP-002 — Work before administration

The Workspace supports operational work. Administrative information is secondary.

### WP-003 — Calm before complexity

The interface should reduce cognitive load. Never overwhelm the user.

### WP-004 — Everything has context

No information shall exist without context. Examples: A task belongs to an experience. A dialogue belongs to a person. A recommendation belongs to an assessment.

### WP-005 — Actions follow understanding

Buttons never come first. Understanding comes first.

### WP-006 — Consistency over novelty

Every Workspace should feel immediately familiar. Users should not have to learn each Workspace separately.

## The Six Operational Blocks

Every Workspace is composed from Operational Blocks.

The content changes. The structure remains familiar.

### Block 1 — Health

Purpose: Provide an immediate operational overview

Question answered: "How is this doing right now?"

Examples: Experience Health, Dialogue Health, Person Overview, Today's Situation

### Block 2 — Work Queue

Purpose: Show operational work waiting

Question answered: "What needs to be done?"

Examples: Follow-up, Phone calls, Accommodation, Planning, Payments

### Block 3 — Timeline

Purpose: Show the time dimension

Question answered: "When does this happen?"

Examples: Calendar, History, Milestones, Deadlines

### Block 4 — Context

Purpose: Provide the information required for professional judgement

Question answered: "What do I need to know?"

Examples: Participants, Dialogues, Documents, Guides, Accommodation, Transport

### Block 5 — Actions

Purpose: Provide meaningful operational actions

Question answered: "What should I do now?"

Actions should be few and relevant.

### Block 6 — Notes

Purpose: Capture operational knowledge

Question answered: "What do we need to remember?"

Notes are working notes, not documents.

## Workspace Consistency

Every Workspace shall answer the same four operational questions:

1. What is the current situation?
2. What needs attention?
3. What do I need to know?
4. What should I do next?

If a Workspace cannot answer these questions, its design should be reconsidered.

## Examples

### Arbetsdag

Health → Work Queue → Planning Calendar → Upcoming Experiences → Quick Actions

### Dialog

Person → Conversation → Timeline → Context → Actions

### Experience

Experience Health → Experience Work Queue → Experience Calendar → Participants → Context → Actions

### Future Person Workspace

Person Overview → Dialogues → Experiences → Timeline → Actions

## Long-Term Goal

All operational work inside AlpenTind Platform shall happen inside Workspaces.

Users should experience the platform as one coherent operational environment, not a collection of pages.
